# Include Render-Backed Presentation Evidence In Formal Polish Scoring

The formal grader will fold screenshot-derived presentation evidence into `visual_audio_polish` instead of keeping it as a separate auxiliary score or a separate top-level score category. The top-level rubric becomes `weapon_controls` 15, `hud_feedback` 8, `trajectory_preview` 17, `projectile_physics` 15, `explosion_gameplay` 20, `visual_audio_polish` 20, and `stability_repeatability` 5; the `visual_audio_polish` pass floor is removed while the trajectory, projectile, explosion, and stability floors remain. Inside `visual_audio_polish`, rendered projectile footprint and rendered explosion footprint are each worth 5 points, projectile asset quality and explosion VFX asset quality are each worth 3 points, detonation audio is worth 2 points, cleanup is worth 1 point, and runtime presentation consistency is worth 1 point.

Rendered footprint checks score screen-space readability, not just existence: too-small projectiles or explosions lose credit, but oversized projectile frames only lose credit when oversize persists across frames or controlled views, and oversized explosions only lose credit when the effect is sustained full-screen or broadly obstructive. The controlled debug-arena capture contributes up to 3 points and the real main-scene capture contributes up to 2 points for each rendered footprint item, so player-facing scene evidence counts without making less deterministic main-scene capture the only hard gate. Runtime asset-quality checks no longer reward mere visibility, avoiding double-counting with rendered footprint evidence. Because render-backed evidence becomes formal scoring evidence, screenshot capture or renderer unavailability is a verifier infrastructure failure rather than a candidate scoring penalty.

The official `run_grader.py` path will add a render-mode option. Full formal scoring that includes render-backed presentation evidence must use a render-capable/windowed mode, while headless runs may remain available for diagnostic or no-screenshot-analysis checks and must not be presented as complete formal scores. If formal screenshot capture is required but unavailable, the run is invalid infrastructure rather than a lower candidate score.

A diagnostic skip path may omit screenshot scoring for fast or headless checks, but its JSON must be marked as diagnostic and incomplete rather than being indistinguishable from a complete formal score. The result should identify omitted formal screenshot components so it cannot be used accidentally in calibration or rollout score tables.

Main-scene rendered evidence distinguishes three cases: renderer or viewport capture failure is verifier infrastructure failure; a candidate main scene that cannot load, expose a playable player, or expose a usable camera is a candidate integration failure; and a captured main scene where grenade visuals are absent or badly sized is a candidate presentation failure.

The score JSON will keep `score/max_score` as the only top-level official score fields, remove the old `logic_score/logic_max_score` aliases, and rename the main score section from `Logic Score` to `Formal Score`. The standalone screenshot probe may continue to emit auxiliary visual-analysis sections for debugging and report evidence, but the formal grader will not expose render-backed presentation points as a separate auxiliary score.
